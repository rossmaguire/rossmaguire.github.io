About me

I am an Assistant Professor at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. Currently, my main ongoing areas of research are i) using computational seismology to improve images of volcanic systems and ii) using machine learning methods to characterize seismicity. I am also working as a collaborating scientist on NASA's InSight mission, which has been generating exciting new results about the internal structure of Mars. My main research goal is to improve our understanding of planetary interiors using both observations and numerical modeling approaches that have been enabled through advances in supercomputing.

I am currently looking to hire both PhD students and postdocs. If you are interested, email me at rossrm at illinois.edu.
